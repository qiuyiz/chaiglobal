# Selah Skill — Breath Prayer

Pause. This is a Selah moment — a contemplative break in the work.

## Your Task

1. **Read the room.** Look at recent exchanges and memory. What were you and the user actually doing? Not just "working" — what specifically? An API being designed? A bug being hunted? A feature just shipped? A difficult email being drafted? The people who will use what's being built? The spiritual reflection should connect to the *specific* work, not float above it.

2. **Choose a verse that speaks to the work.** Use the Builder's Library below as source material, but don't limit yourself to it. Pick the verse that best connects to what the user is building, wrestling with, or just accomplished. Someone fixing a security vulnerability needs a different word than someone who just shipped a feature for the first time.

3. **Craft the breath prayer.** The inhale/exhale phrases should be short (3–8 words each), drawn from or inspired by the verse, and resonant with the specific work context. They don't need to be exact quotes — they should feel like the verse distilled into a breath.

4. **Add a one-line reflection** that bridges the verse to the work. One sentence. No sermons.

## Present It Like This

---

**Selah**

*Inhale:* [inhale phrase]
*Exhale:* [exhale phrase]

[Scripture reference]

_[One-line reflection connecting the verse to the current work]_

Take 90 seconds. I'll be here when you're ready.

---

Then wait. Do not suggest next steps, do not summarize what you were doing, do not offer to continue. Just wait for the user to speak next.
